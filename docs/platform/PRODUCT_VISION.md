# Product vision

Vynlo gives small and medium dealerships one operational system for inventory, vehicle media, website merchandising, leads, customers, deals, documents, and reporting. It replaces fragmented spreadsheets, shared folders, disconnected website CMS records, and manually repeated paperwork.

The first release is optimized for independent used-vehicle dealerships. The data model also supports new vehicles.

## Target dealership

A typical Vynlo customer:

- operates one or several locations;
- acquires vehicles for retail or wholesale resale;
- mostly completes cash sales;
- sometimes arranges financing through an outside lender;
- stores photos and purchase/sale documents;
- publishes listings to a website and later multiple channels;
- needs simple CRM, tasks, appointments, and reporting;
- has limited or no internal engineering staff.

## Product promise

A staff member should be able to create and prepare inventory, optimize and publish photos/listing data, capture and follow a lead, create a cash or externally financed deal, generate tenant-approved documents, record one-time transactions, close the sale, and preserve a complete audit trail.

## Relationship to vAuto-style products

Vynlo's MVP is comparable only in the broad category of centralized inventory operations and merchandising. It does not claim proprietary market pricing, appraisal intelligence, sourcing, competitive-market data, or dealer-network syndication in MVP. Those require separate data sources, licensing, and modules.

## First tenant

Drivven validates configurability through versioned workspace configuration and a non-secret bootstrap seed. Its unusual workflows exercise generic engines without redefining the platform's normal customer experience.
